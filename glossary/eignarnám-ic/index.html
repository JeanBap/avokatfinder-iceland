Eignarnám (Expropriation)

Compulsory acquisition of private property by the Icelandic state

Eignarnám is the compulsory acquisition of private real estate by the Icelandic state or a public body for a public purpose — roads, infrastructure, utilities, or national interest projects. Iceland's Constitution (Stjórnarskrá) guarantees the right to property and requires full compensation (sanngjarnar bætur) when property is compulsorily acquired.

Expropriation in Iceland is governed by the Expropriation Act (lög um framkvæmd eignarnáms). It can only occur for a legitimate public purpose, and the expropriating authority must demonstrate that the public benefit justifies the interference with private property rights. A formal process of negotiation before compulsory acquisition is required.

How It Works in Practice

Compensation for expropriated property must reflect the property's full market value at the time of acquisition. If the owner and the expropriating authority cannot agree on the amount, the matter is referred to an independent Expropriation Commission (eignarnámsnefnd), whose decision can be appealed to the courts.

Buyers should check whether a property is designated or reserved for a public infrastructure project before purchasing. Icelandic municipalities' aðalskipulag (master plan) and national transport authority plans indicate areas reserved for roads, utilities, or other public uses.

Energy ProjectsGeothermal and infrastructure expansion — rural land risk

Iceland's ongoing geothermal energy development and infrastructure expansion mean that some rural and suburban properties may be in the path of planned projects. Buyers of rural land in particular should check for any national or municipal designations affecting the parcel.

Common Mistake: Buyers of rural land sometimes overlook the possibility of infrastructure designation. Iceland's energy and transport infrastructure is actively expanding, and some rural parcels are reserved for future projects. Check the relevant municipal and national plans before purchasing.
Expert Tip: Request a planning confirmation (skipulagsupplýsingar) from the local authority confirming that the property is not designated for public acquisition or reserved for infrastructure. This is a standard due diligence step for any significant purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can the Icelandic state take my property?

Yes, but only for a legitimate public purpose and with payment of full market value compensation. Iceland's Constitution and the Expropriation Act govern the process.

Who decides the compensation amount if we disagree?

The Expropriation Commission (eignarnámsnefnd) determines compensation if the parties cannot agree. Its decision can be appealed to the district courts.

How do I check if my property is designated for expropriation?

Review the municipal master plan (aðalskipulag) and national infrastructure plans. Request a planning confirmation (skipulagsupplýsingar) from the local authority.
