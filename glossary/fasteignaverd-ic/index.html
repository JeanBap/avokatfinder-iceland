Fasteignaverð og Verðlag (Property Prices and the Market)

Understanding Iceland's real estate market prices and trends

Fasteignaverð (property price) in Iceland — particularly in Reykjavík and the Capital Region — is among the highest relative to local incomes in Europe. Understanding the drivers of Iceland's property market is essential for any buyer.

Iceland's housing market is characterised by very limited supply, driven by geographic constraints, planning restrictions, and the small population base. Reykjavík and its suburbs are where approximately 65% of Iceland's 380,000 residents live, creating intense competition for limited housing stock.

How It Works in Practice

The fasteignamat (official assessed value) published by Þjóðskrá Íslands is updated regularly and used as a reference point for taxation and mortgages, but market prices often deviate significantly. Buyers should research actual transacted prices rather than relying on fasteignamat figures.

Tourism has significantly affected Iceland's property market, with short-term rental platforms reducing the supply of long-term residential rental accommodation. Reykjavík's regulatory responses to short-term rentals have evolved and should be checked before buying a property for rental purposes.

Key Facts

MarketLimited supply; high demand; prices high relative to income
Population~380,000 total; ~65% in Capital Region
FasteignamatOfficial assessed value — may diverge from market price
Short-Term RentalsAirbnb regulation in Reykjavík — check before buying for rental
InflationHigh historically; indexed mortgages add complexity

Iceland's inflation has historically been higher than in many European countries. The indexation of mortgage debt to the CPI adds another layer of complexity — buyers should model the total cost of ownership including both mortgage indexation and expected price appreciation.

Common Mistake: Buyers who use the fasteignamat as a proxy for market value may misjudge their offer. In a rising market, actual transaction prices can be significantly higher than the assessed value.
Expert Tip: Ask your agent for a comparable sales analysis (samanburðargreiðslur) showing actual transacted prices for similar properties over the past six months before making an offer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Iceland's real estate market expensive?

Yes. Prices in Reykjavík and the Capital Region are among the highest relative to local incomes in Europe, driven by limited supply and strong demand.

What is the fasteignamat?

The official assessed value published by Þjóðskrá Íslands, used as a basis for taxation and mortgages. Market prices can deviate significantly.

How do short-term rental regulations affect buyers in Iceland?

Reykjavík limits short-term rentals. Buyers intending to use a property as an Airbnb should verify current rules before purchasing.
