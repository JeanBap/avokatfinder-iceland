Húsaskoðun (Property Survey)

A professional property condition survey in Iceland

Húsaskoðun is a professional inspection of a property's physical condition, conducted by a licensed building inspector (húsaskoðunarmaður) or structural engineer before purchase in Iceland. Given Iceland's unique geological environment and older building stock, a professional survey is essential.

Surveys typically assess the structural integrity of the building, condition of the roof, windows, and external walls, state of plumbing, electrical, and heating systems, presence of damp or mould, and any signs of foundation issues. An energy efficiency assessment (orkumat) is often included.

How It Works in Practice

Iceland's building stock includes many properties built in the mid-20th century with materials that may require significant ongoing investment. Concrete cancer (concrete decay from rebar corrosion) is a known issue in some older Icelandic buildings and requires specialist assessment where suspected.

The survey report provides an independent assessment of the property's condition and estimated remediation costs. It is a critical tool for price renegotiation — most kaupmáli agreements allow withdrawal or renegotiation on the basis of survey findings.

Survey WindowCommission within the kaupmáli survey period

Survey companies (húsaskoðunarfyrirtæki) operating in Iceland can be engaged directly by buyers. Costs typically range from 50,000 to 150,000 ISK depending on property size and complexity — modest relative to property values.

Common Mistake: Buyers who skip the survey to save money or move quickly risk discovering expensive defects after purchase. On a property worth tens of millions of ISK, a survey is always worthwhile.
Expert Tip: Request the survey report before the end of the kaupmáli survey period. Material defects found in time allow withdrawal or renegotiation within the contractually protected window.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does a húsaskoðun cover?

Structural integrity, roof, windows, walls, plumbing, electrical systems, heating, and energy efficiency. Specialist checks may be needed for older buildings.

What is concrete cancer in Icelandic buildings?

It is the corrosion and expansion of steel rebar within concrete, causing cracking and spalling. It affects some older Icelandic buildings and requires specialist structural assessment.

How much does a húsaskoðun cost?

Typically 50,000 to 150,000 ISK depending on property size — modest compared to the cost of undetected defects.
