Kaupmáli (Purchase Agreement / Pre-Contract)

A binding preliminary purchase agreement in Iceland

Kaupmáli refers to a binding preliminary purchase agreement — a contract signed between buyer and seller that commits both parties to the transaction at an agreed price and on agreed terms, before the final formal transfer (kaupsamningur) is executed. In Iceland, the kaupmáli is often as legally binding as the final contract.

The kaupmáli typically commits the buyer to pay a deposit (handsal, commonly 10% of the purchase price) within a short period of signing. The deposit is held by the agent in a client account and is at risk if the buyer withdraws without legal justification — the seller may be entitled to retain it as liquidated damages.

How It Works in Practice

Standard kaupmáli templates are used by most Icelandic estate agents and contain conditions precedent — typically a mortgage financing condition allowing the buyer to withdraw if mortgage approval cannot be obtained within a specified period (usually five to ten working days). Buyers should ensure this condition is clearly included.

The kaupmáli also typically includes a condition allowing the buyer to commission a technical survey of the property (húsaskoðun) within a defined period. If the survey reveals material defects not disclosed by the seller, the buyer may be entitled to withdraw or renegotiate the price.

Key Facts

DepositHandsal — typically 10% of price; held in agent client account
Buyer WithdrawalDeposit at risk if buyer withdraws without legal justification
Finance ConditionStandard condition allowing withdrawal if mortgage not approved
Survey WindowHúsaskoðun period — buyer can inspect and withdraw on material defects
Binding EffectBoth parties bound; withdrawal attracts significant liability

Once signed, the kaupmáli binds both parties. Sellers who withdraw after signing are liable to the buyer for damages, typically including return of the deposit plus additional compensation. Buyers contemplating withdrawal should seek legal advice before acting, as the consequences can be significant.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes sign the kaupmáli on the day of the viewing without taking time to read it carefully or check the financing condition wording. Always read the full document and confirm the finance condition is clear and the survey period is adequate before signing.
Expert Tip: Use the full survey period allowed in the kaupmáli to commission an independent technical inspection. This is your last protected opportunity to withdraw on the basis of property condition before the final contract locks you in completely.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the kaupmáli?

It is a binding preliminary purchase agreement in Iceland, committing buyer and seller to the transaction at an agreed price. It is as legally binding as the final transfer contract.

What is the handsal?

It is the deposit — typically 10% of the purchase price — paid by the buyer when signing the kaupmáli and held by the agent. It is at risk if the buyer withdraws without justification.

Can I withdraw from a kaupmáli after signing?

Only on grounds specified in the kaupmáli — such as failure to obtain financing or material defects found during the survey period. Withdrawal outside these grounds risks forfeiting the handsal and further damages.
