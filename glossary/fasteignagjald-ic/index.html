Fasteignagjald (Property Tax)

The annual municipal property tax levied on real estate in Iceland

Fasteignagjald is the annual property tax levied by Icelandic municipalities on the owners of real estate. It is calculated as a percentage of the property's official assessed value (fasteignamat), which is maintained by Þjóðskrá Íslands (Registers Iceland). The tax is a primary revenue source for municipalities.

Each municipality sets its own fasteignagjald rate, within bounds set by national legislation. Rates in Reykjavík and surrounding municipalities typically range from approximately 0.5% to 0.8% of the fasteignamat for residential properties. Properties used for commercial purposes face different rates.

How It Works in Practice

The fasteignagjald is invoiced annually (usually at the start of the year) and can be paid in instalments. Late payment attracts interest. Property tax is a charge against the property itself — unpaid tax can accumulate as a lien (veðréttur) that must be settled before the property can be cleanly transferred.

Buyers should verify whether any fasteignagjald arrears are outstanding before purchasing. A fresh search of the land register and a clearance certificate from the municipality confirm that no tax lien has been registered against the property.

Certain categories of property or owner may qualify for reductions or exemptions — for example, agricultural land may face a different regime, and religious or charitable organisations may benefit from full exemption. Buyers of converted or mixed-use properties should confirm the applicable rate.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes forget to check for fasteignagjald arrears, assuming they will be mentioned by the agent. Unpaid property tax silently accumulates as a lien that transfers with the property. Always request a tax clearance certificate.
Expert Tip: Ask the municipality for a written confirmation that all fasteignagjald payments are current before signing the purchase contract. This document is quick to obtain and definitively protects you against inheriting unpaid tax.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is fasteignagjald?

It is Iceland's annual municipal property tax, calculated as a percentage of the property's official assessed value (fasteignamat).

Who sets the fasteignagjald rate?

Each Icelandic municipality sets its own rate within limits set by national legislation. Rates vary between municipalities.

Can unpaid property tax create a lien in Iceland?

Yes. Outstanding fasteignagjald accumulates as a veðréttur (lien) registered against the property, which must be settled before a clean title transfer can take place.
