Sameignarfélag og Húsfélag

Shared property and homeowners associations in Iceland

Sameignarfélag refers to a joint ownership arrangement — typically the co-ownership of common parts of a multi-unit building. Húsfélag is the homeowners association that manages shared property and collective decision-making for apartment buildings in Iceland.

Every apartment owner in a multi-unit building is automatically a member of the húsfélag. The association manages common areas — staircases, lifts, roofs, basements, and shared heating systems — and levies monthly maintenance fees (húsfélagsgjald) proportional to each unit's size or value.

How It Works in Practice

The húsfélag is governed by an elected committee (stjórn) and holds an annual general meeting (ársfundur) at which members vote on maintenance budgets, major works, and committee appointments. Simple majority for ordinary maintenance; supermajority for large expenditures.

Before purchasing an apartment, buyers should request the húsfélag's accounts (ársreikningar), recent meeting minutes (fundargerðir), and written correspondence about planned major works. A healthy reserve fund (viðhaldssjóður) indicates a well-managed building.

Key Facts

MembershipAutomatic on apartment purchase
Monthly FeeHúsfélagsgjald — proportional to unit size or value
GovernanceElected committee (stjórn) + annual meeting (ársfundur)
Due DiligenceRequest accounts, minutes, reserve fund balance
HeatingMany buildings on hitaveita (district heating)

Iceland's high energy costs mean heating system efficiency is an important factor in apartment purchases. Many Reykjavík buildings are connected to the municipal district heating system (hitaveita), providing reliable and cost-effective heating.

Common Mistake: Buyers who purchase apartments without reviewing húsfélag accounts may face large special assessments for roof or heating system replacement shortly after purchase. A low reserve fund is a warning sign.
Expert Tip: Ask the committee specifically about planned major works in the next five years. Planned projects not yet in the accounts can be a significant unexpected cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a húsfélag?

A homeowners association managing the common areas of a multi-unit building in Iceland. Every apartment owner is automatically a member.

What is húsfélagsgjald?

The monthly maintenance fee levied by the húsfélag on each apartment owner, covering common area maintenance and the building's reserve fund.

What is the hitaveita?

Iceland's municipal district heating system, providing hot water heating to most Reykjavík buildings from geothermal sources.
