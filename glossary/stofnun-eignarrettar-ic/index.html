Stofnun Eignarréttarins (Title Registration)

How property ownership is formally established in Iceland's land register

Stofnun eignarréttarins (establishment of the ownership right) refers to the process of formally registering a property purchase in Iceland's official land register (Þinglýsingabók), maintained by Þjóðskrá Íslands. Registration makes the new owner's title effective against third parties.

The purchase contract (kaupsamningur) is the primary document submitted for registration. It must contain the kennitala of both parties, the property's fasteignanúmer, the agreed price, and any conditions. The agent typically submits the registration request electronically through Þjóðskrá's system.

How It Works in Practice

Iceland's land register operates on the priority-by-registration-date principle (þinglýsingardagur). The first to register has priority over later registrations — buyers should submit the registration request immediately after signing.

Transfer documents submitted for registration are subject to stamp duty (þinglýsingargjald) — a fee calculated as a percentage of the property's registered value. This is one of the ancillary costs buyers must budget for.

Electronic FilingStandard for agents in Iceland

Þjóðskrá Íslands provides online access to the land register (fasteignaskrá), allowing anyone to verify the current owner and check for registered encumbrances — a powerful due diligence tool buyers should use before making any offer.

Common Mistake: Buyers who delay submitting the registration request are vulnerable to the seller's creditors registering a lien first. Register immediately after signing the kaupsamningur.
Expert Tip: Run an online search of the fasteignaskrá on the morning of signing to confirm no encumbrances have been registered since initial due diligence. The system is updated in real time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Þinglýsingabók?

Iceland's official land register maintained by Þjóðskrá Íslands, recording all registered property rights including ownership and encumbrances.

What is þinglýsingargjald?

The stamp duty payable when a transfer document is submitted to the land register, calculated as a percentage of the property's registered value.

Can I check property ownership online in Iceland?

Yes — Þjóðskrá Íslands provides public access to the fasteignaskrá where anyone can verify the current registered owner and encumbrances.
