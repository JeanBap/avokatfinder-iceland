Sameignarfélag og Húsfélag

Shared property and homeowners associations in Iceland

Sameignarfélag refers to a joint ownership arrangement — typically the co-ownership of common parts of a multi-unit building — in Iceland. Húsfélag is the homeowners association that manages shared property, common areas, and collective decision-making for apartment buildings.

Every apartment owner in a multi-unit building is automatically a member of the húsfélag. The association manages the building's common areas — staircases, lifts, roofs, basements, and shared heating systems — and levies monthly maintenance fees (húsfélagsgjald) on each member proportional to their unit's size or value quotient.

How It Works in Practice

The húsfélag is governed by an elected committee (stjórn) and holds an annual general meeting (ársfundur) at which members vote on maintenance budgets, major works, and committee appointments. Decisions on ordinary maintenance typically require a simple majority; structural changes or large expenditures may require a supermajority.

Before purchasing an apartment, buyers should request the húsfélag's accounts (ársreikningar), recent meeting minutes (fundargerðir), and any written correspondence about planned major works. A healthy reserve fund (viðhaldssjóður) indicates a well-managed building and reduces the risk of special assessments after purchase.

Key Facts

MembershipAutomatic on apartment purchase
Monthly FeeHúsfélagsgjald — proportional to unit size or value
GovernanceElected committee (stjórn) + annual meeting (ársfundur)
Due DiligenceRequest accounts, minutes, reserve fund balance
HeatingMany buildings on hitaveita (district heating) — efficient but ongoing cost

Iceland's high energy costs — despite the country's abundance of geothermal energy — mean that heating system efficiency is an important factor in apartment purchases. Many Reykjavík buildings are connected to the municipal district heating system (hitaveita), which provides reliable and cost-effective heating but is still a significant monthly expense.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes purchase apartments without reviewing the húsfélag accounts, only to discover a large special assessment for roof or heating system replacement shortly after. A low reserve fund is a warning sign that should prompt a price reduction negotiation.
Expert Tip: At the annual meeting or through the committee, ask specifically about any planned major works in the next five years. Planned projects not yet reflected in the accounts can be a significant unexpected cost for a new owner.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a húsfélag?

It is a homeowners association that manages the common areas and collective affairs of a multi-unit building in Iceland. Every apartment owner is automatically a member.

What is húsfélagsgjald?

It is the monthly maintenance fee levied by the húsfélag on each apartment owner, covering common area maintenance, insurance, and the building's reserve fund.

What is the hitaveita?

It is Iceland's municipal district heating system, which provides hot water heating to most Reykjavík buildings from geothermal sources. It is reliable and relatively cost-effective compared to other heating methods.
