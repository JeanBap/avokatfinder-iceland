Gjaldtökureglur og Skattlagning (Taxes and Fees on Property)

Overview of property-related taxes and fees in Iceland

Iceland imposes several taxes and fees on real estate transactions and ownership. Understanding the full tax picture before purchasing is essential to accurate budgeting. The main property-related impositions are the fasteignagjald (municipal property tax), þinglýsingargjald (land register stamp duty), capital gains tax (fjármagnstekjuskattur), and VAT on new builds.

Capital gains on property sales by individuals are subject to fjármagnstekjuskattur (capital income tax) at a flat rate of 22%. Gains are calculated as the difference between the sale price and the acquisition cost, adjusted for inflation using the consumer price index. The primary residence exemption reduces or eliminates this liability for many sellers.

How It Works in Practice

Sellers who have owned and occupied a property as their primary home for at least two years are exempt from capital gains tax on that property. This exemption is significant and explains why many Icelanders hold their primary home rather than investment properties.

New residential construction is subject to VAT (virðisaukaskattur) at 25% on the construction value. Buyers of new apartments from developers pay the VAT as part of the purchase price — the quoted price for a new build typically includes VAT. Resales of existing properties are generally VAT-exempt.

Iceland's property tax system is relatively simple compared to many European countries, reflecting the country's broader low-tax philosophy for capital income. However, the high headline capital gains tax rate of 22% on investment property is significant for non-primary residence investors.

Common Mistake: Investors sometimes forget that the capital gains tax exemption applies only to the primary residence — investment properties, holiday homes, and rental properties are all subject to 22% capital gains tax on sale. Factor this into return calculations before purchasing an investment property.
Expert Tip: If you are a seller claiming the primary residence capital gains exemption, confirm with an Icelandic skattaráðgjafi (tax adviser) that you meet the two-year occupation requirement. An error in claiming the exemption can result in a large unexpected tax bill.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the capital gains tax rate on property in Iceland?

22% (fjármagnstekjuskattur) on the inflation-adjusted gain. The primary residence is exempt if owned and occupied for at least two years.

Is VAT charged on property purchases in Iceland?

VAT at 25% applies to new residential construction. Resales of existing properties are generally VAT-exempt.

When is the primary residence exempt from capital gains tax?

The seller must have owned and occupied the property as their primary residence for at least two years immediately before the sale.
