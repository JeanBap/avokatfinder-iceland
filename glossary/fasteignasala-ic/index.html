Fasteignasala (Real Estate Agency)

A licensed real estate agency facilitating property sales in Iceland

Fasteignasala refers to a licensed real estate brokerage in Iceland. All practising real estate agents (fasteignasalar) must hold a licence from the Financial Supervisory Authority (Fjármálaeftirlitið / FME) under the Act on Real Estate Brokers (lög um fasteignasölu). This licensing requirement distinguishes Iceland's agent sector from many smaller European markets.

Licensed agents are required to maintain professional indemnity insurance, follow strict disclosure rules, and keep transaction funds in segregated client accounts. They act as intermediaries in property sales, preparing the purchase contract, coordinating with the land register, and ensuring both parties meet their contractual obligations.

How It Works in Practice

Real estate agent fees (þóknun fasteignasala) in Iceland are typically paid by the seller and range from 1.5% to 2.5% of the purchase price plus VAT (25%). The fee should be agreed in writing before the agent begins marketing the property. In a competitive market, some fees are negotiable.

Iceland's real estate market is dominated by a handful of large agencies, with Eignamiðlun, Fasteignir, and Húsasmiðjan among the better-known names. Online listing portals such as mbl.is and fasteignir.is are the primary search tools for buyers.

Property transactions in Iceland are generally conducted entirely through licensed agents and lawyers (lögmenn). For complex transactions — particularly for foreign buyers unfamiliar with Icelandic law — engaging an independent lawyer in addition to the agent is strongly recommended.

Common Mistake: Buyers sometimes assume the agent is looking out for their interests. Icelandic agents represent the seller. While they must act fairly, buyers should commission their own legal review of the purchase contract.
Expert Tip: Run all prospective properties through Þjóðskrá Íslands (Registers Iceland) to verify ownership and any registered charges before making an offer. The FME also maintains a public register of licensed agents — verify your agent's licence is current.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are all real estate agents in Iceland licensed?

Yes. Agents must hold a licence from the Financial Supervisory Authority (FME) under the Act on Real Estate Brokers. Check the FME's public register.

Who pays the agent's fee in Iceland?

The seller typically pays the agent's commission, which ranges from 1.5% to 2.5% of the sale price plus 25% VAT.

Can I buy in Iceland without using an agent?

Technically yes, but most transactions use a licensed agent. Foreign buyers in particular benefit from an agent's market knowledge, combined with an independent lawyer for legal review.
