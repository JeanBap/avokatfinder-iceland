Leigusamningur (Lease Agreement)

A residential or commercial lease agreement in Iceland

Leigusamningur is the term for a lease or rental agreement in Iceland. Residential tenancies are governed by the Act on Residential Leases (lög um húsaleigusamninga), which provides substantial tenant protections and sets mandatory rules that cannot be waived to the tenant's detriment.

Residential lease agreements in Iceland must be in writing for terms exceeding one month. The agreement must specify the monthly rent, lease term (fixed or open-ended), security deposit (tryggingagreiðsla), notice periods, and the landlord and tenant's respective maintenance obligations. Open-ended leases are the most common form.

How It Works in Practice

Security deposits in Iceland are capped at three months' rent for residential tenancies. The deposit must be returned within thirty days of the tenancy ending, less any documented deductions for damage beyond normal wear. Unjustified deductions can be challenged before the Leigustefna (Tenancy Tribunal).

Icelandic tenants have strong rights: the landlord may only terminate an open-ended residential tenancy on specific legal grounds and with at least three months' notice. Tenants who dispute a termination can apply to the Leigustefna, which has authority to declare unlawful terminations void.

Iceland's rental market — particularly in Reykjavík — is very tight, with high demand from tourism workers, students, and new arrivals. Rents have risen significantly in recent years. Tenants who find suitable accommodation should ensure they have a signed written agreement before paying any deposit.

Common Mistake: Tenants sometimes pay deposits before signing a written lease, relying on the landlord's word. In Iceland's competitive rental market, this is risky — without a signed agreement, the tenant's rights are very limited if a dispute arises.
Expert Tip: Before signing any lease, verify the landlord's ownership of the property through Þjóðskrá Íslands. This prevents fraudulent lettings where someone purports to let a property they do not own.

Frequently Asked Questions

What law governs residential leases in Iceland?

The Act on Residential Leases (lög um húsaleigusamninga) governs residential tenancies, providing mandatory protections for tenants.

What is the Leigustefna?

It is Iceland's Tenancy Tribunal — an administrative body that resolves disputes between landlords and tenants over leases, deposits, terminations, and rent.

Can a landlord terminate a residential lease without notice in Iceland?

No. Open-ended residential tenancies can only be terminated on specific legal grounds and with at least three months' notice. Unlawful terminations can be challenged before the Leigustefna.
