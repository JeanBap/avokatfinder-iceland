Veð

Definition: Icelandic mortgage or security interest registered against real property.

Overview

Veð is the Icelandic term for a mortgage or security interest in real property. When a property owner borrows money, the lender typically requires veð to secure the loan with the property as collateral.

A veð is registered in the fasteignabálið (property register) and creates a legal claim against the property. Upon borrower default, the lender can initiate foreclosure to seize and sell the property.

Multiple veðir (plural of veð) can be registered against the same property, each with priority determined by registration date. First-registered mortgages have priority in receiving proceeds from property sales.

Icelandic law limits the amount of veð a lender can require, typically to the property's assessed value. Lenders must follow strict regulations regarding disclosure and documentation of mortgage terms.

Once a veð is satisfied, the lender must formally discharge it from the property register. Until discharged, the lender retains a legal claim against the property, even if the underlying debt has been paid.

Key Facts

  • Mortgage or security interest on Icelandic property
  • Registered in fasteignabálið
  • Creates priority claim if property foreclosed
  • Amount limited to property value
  • Must be discharged when loan is satisfied

Common Mistake

Watch out: Not fully understanding the terms of veð, including interest rates, payment schedules, and foreclosure procedures.

Pro Tip

💡 Review all veð documents carefully with an Icelandic lawyer before committing to mortgage debt.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can multiple veðir be registered on one property?

Yes. Multiple mortgages may be registered, with priority determined by registration order.

What rights does a lender have if I default on veð?

The lender can initiate foreclosure proceedings to sell the property and recover the debt from sale proceeds.

How do I remove veð after paying my mortgage?

Request the lender formally discharge (afskrá) the mortgage from the fasteignabálið register.
