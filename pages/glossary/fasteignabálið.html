Fasteignabálið

Definition: Icelandic property register that records ownership and encumbrances on real estate.

Overview

Fasteignabálið is Iceland's official property register maintained by the Icelandic Land Registry (Landskrá), recording property ownership and all legal interests in real estate. It provides transparent, publicly accessible evidence of property rights.

Each Icelandic property receives a unique register number that remains constant regardless of ownership changes. This system ensures clarity and prevents disputes over property identification.

The fasteignabálið records the current owner, any mortgages (veð), liens, easements, and restrictions on the property. This comprehensive recording protects both property owners and creditors by clearly documenting all encumbrances.

When property changes hands in Iceland, the transfer must be registered in the fasteignabálið. This registration is essential to establish legal ownership and ensure the new owner's rights are publicly recognized.

The fasteignabálið is open to public inspection, allowing prospective buyers, creditors, and interested parties to verify property ownership and identify any mortgages or other claims before engaging in transactions.

Key Facts

  • Maintained by Icelandic Land Registry (Landskrá)
  • Each property has unique register number
  • Records ownership, mortgages, liens, easements
  • Provides public access to property information
  • Updated when ownership or encumbrances change

Common Mistake

Watch out: Not checking the fasteignabálið before purchasing Icelandic property, potentially buying land with hidden mortgages.

Pro Tip

💡 Always request and review an official extract from Landskrá before purchasing to verify clear title.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I access information in the Icelandic fasteignabálið?

Contact Landskrá (Icelandic Land Registry) with the property address or register number to request an official extract.

What information appears in the fasteignabálið?

Current owner, mortgages, liens, easements, and other registered restrictions or legal interests affecting the property.

How long does it take to register a property transfer in Iceland?

Typically 2-4 weeks after proper documents are submitted to the Land Registry.
